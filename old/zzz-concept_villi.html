What are intestinal villi?

You don’t need a degree in medicine to understand that your intestines have villi, and these villi absorb nutrients from the foods you digest in your stomach. If you don’t clean off the villi in your intestinal tract, they can’t absorb nutrients well.

Try this:
Hold your arms out in front of you with your fingers pointed at each other.

Your intestines play a major role in digestion, transforming the food you eat into the different fuel bits for your body to use. The villi cover your intestines and their job is to absorb nutrients from your digested food and transport those nutrients to be used by your body.

Wiggle your fingers. This is what your intestinal villi looks like. These are like your “villi fingers.”

The villi works when the surface area is exposed to food.

Now turn on the faucet and hold your “villi fingers” under the stream of water. Notice the surface area of your fingers making contact with the water. With 100% contact you get wet/clean hands. (Capt. Obvious)

This is how intestinal villi “work”. The stomach digests your food and as the digested food, called “chyme” enters your intestines it flows over your villi. The surface area of the villi absorbs nutrients from the chyme. As we learned in Nature's Rake section, insoluble fiber moves through the intestines keeping the path clean by sweeping away deposits left by previous digestion cycles.

Theory -- nutrition malfunction

What happens if we put glue on our fingers and run them under the water? In some areas, the glue prevents the water from making contact with your fingers’ surface.

We just learned that nutrient absorption (getting the most from what you eat) depends on surface area contact with the digested food. We also learned how fiber sweeps our intestinal passage clean. making sure the villi has a clean surface area to do its job.

Conclusion

Let’s apply this thinking to the act of eating an orange.
If someone with healthy intestines eats an orange, they gain X amt of vitamin C. A person with intestinal deposits obscuring nutrient absorption will get a fraction of the nutrition and need to eat more food to gain the same level of needed vitamin C.
The irony is that one may be obese because one is, in fact, starving to death.
