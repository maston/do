Step 1: The Food Journal

There is an incredible rise in research about food journaling and food journal software in the past few years. (find some academic links) Pick one that you feel comfortable using consistently. For a list of good food journals see Tools(link).
If changing eating patterns is the sport, the food journal is the raquette. It is hard to fix a problem without information around that problem.
It’s important to not think of what we are about to do here as “a diet.” The truth is it’s not. You are going to spend some time getting situated in a new habit. This one can be easily done at work.

Food Journalling will become your biggest friend for a while.

You will need:

A food journaling application. Like MyFitnessPal or MyPlate.

Your Task:

Use a food journal to start seeing what kind of problem we are dealing with.

Duration:

For 2 weeks to a month, I want you to start getting into the habit of food journaling.

Purpose:

Creating a baseline. Building your self awareness of what you eat. Wrapping your head around what’s going on with your eating patterns.

What to look for:

When I started this process 3 years ago, I was focused on one component. Fiber.
Knowing what you now know about Fiber, start to look at “How much fiber do I get normally? From where?”
We all know about sodium and heart disease and how it contributes to dehydration (link).
"What does your sodium intake look like?”

The usual suspects for high sodium foods are processed foods, deli meats, and canned soups.

When you have successfully food journaled for 2 weeks, move to Step 2.
Note: If you want to do Step 2 as you learn to food journal, that’s okay too.
