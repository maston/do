The Relational Nature of Food

cancer cell photo credit: Giant Microbes

If you give your body the tools it needs, it is smarter than you think and will heal itself.

For example, by giving my body oatmeal I am delivering tools in the form of soluble and insoluble fiber to my intestines and liver. My intestines will take this resource and use it to cleanse itself and construct a gel out of the soluble fiber that will bind with old bile, pulling cholesterol out of my blood stream and triggering the liver to make new bile. The effect of eating oatmeal is to improve intestinal villi function (affects nutrition absorption), refresh my bile (affects fat digestion) and lower my cholesterol level.
This is the concept behind "The Ambrosia Project" which is now being developed by me and my team at IBM. We will present this technology in October, 2016 in Las Vegas at the IBM World of Watson Conference.

HBO Documentary Films: The Weight of the Nation

The message from this four-part HBO Documentary is “To win, we have to lose.” I feel this is required viewing if you really want to wrap your head around what is going on. Each film explores a topic, ranging from the social aspects of obesity to the physiology of what is going wrong with the human body. Lessons from top doctors, sociologists and psychologists delivering everything you need to know to get up to speed on where current research and thinking is at.

Food Matters Movie

“Food Matters” makes the case that you can heal yourself via nutrition. It drives home the concept we have all grown up with: “You are what you eat.” The current leaders in integrative health and practitioners of traditional western medicine team up to explain the “Sickness Industry” that is profiting on making pills for everything, when in reality, you need to look to your food. A fascinating exploration into vitamins and food vs. pharmaceuticals.

Killer At Large

“Killer at Large" is an eye opening journey into how the Obesity Epidemic got here. From the rise of industrialized farming and supermarkets, to the overabundance of energy-rich (high calorie)/ low-nutrition foods. A compelling argument to former Surgeon General of the United States Dr. Richard Carmona’s statement that obesity is the most pressing issue before him.
